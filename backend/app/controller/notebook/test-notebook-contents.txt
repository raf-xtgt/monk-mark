Note1: "The Deep Work Hypothesis: The ability to perform deep work is becoming increasingly rare at exactly the same time it is becoming increasingly valuable in our economy. As a consequence, the few who cultivate this skill, and then make it the core of their working life, will thrive."

Note2: This feels like a direct challenge. I spend so much time in "reactive" mode—answering messages and fixing minor bugs—that I wonder if I’m actually building the "rare" value Newport is talking about.

Note3: "Shallow Work: Noncognitively demanding, logistical-style tasks, often performed while distracted. These efforts tend not to create much new value in the world and are easy to replicate."

Note4: "Easy to replicate" is the scary part. If 80% of my day is Slack and email, is my career essentially replaceable by anyone with a decent internet connection?
